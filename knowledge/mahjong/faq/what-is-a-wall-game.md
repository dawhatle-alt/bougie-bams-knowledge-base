# What Is A Wall Game?

## Summary

A wall game is a hand that ends with no winner: the wall is exhausted, the final discard is made, and no one has declared mahjong. In most social play no money changes hands and the deal simply rotates.

## Details

When the last wall tile is drawn and the final discard draws no mahjong call, the hand is over. Standard social practice treats it as a clean reset — shuffle and move the deal. Some groups roll a kitty to the next winner, and some tournament formats give a small bonus to players whose hands were still viable while dead hands receive nothing.

## Examples

- Four defensive players run the wall out; nobody pays, and the next dealer builds new walls.
- A league table awards viable hands a flat bonus after a wall game.

## Common Mistakes

- Paying out on a wall game when the group's standard is no exchange.
- Forgetting that forcing a wall game is a legitimate defensive outcome.
