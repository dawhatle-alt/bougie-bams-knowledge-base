# Can I Exchange A Joker From A Dead Hand?

## Summary

Yes, if the joker sits in a valid exposure made before the hand went dead. No, if the joker is part of the invalid exposure that killed the hand, or if the dead player was attempting a concealed hand.

## Details

Valid exposures made before disqualification stay on top of the dead player's rack, and active players may redeem those jokers with the matching natural tile on their turn — the dead player must make the swap when asked. Jokers inside the errant exposure that caused the dead hand return to the rack and are forfeited. A dead concealed hand returns entirely to the rack, so none of its jokers can be swapped.

## Examples

- A dead player's earlier valid kong with a joker: an active player exchanges a natural tile for it normally.
- A joker in the invalid exposure that caused the dead hand: unavailable for the rest of the round.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating every joker on a dead player's rack as forfeited.
- Trying to redeem a joker from a dead concealed hand.
