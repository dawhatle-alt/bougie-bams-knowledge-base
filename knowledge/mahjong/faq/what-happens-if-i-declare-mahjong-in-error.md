# What Happens If I Declare Mahjong In Error?

## Summary

If you have exposed nothing, you may take the call back with no penalty. If you exposed only the claimed tile or one exposure, you may retract the win but that exposure stays. If you exposed more of your hand, your hand is dead.

## Details

The severity scales with what the table has seen. A verbal call alone is retractable. One committed exposure locks that exposure but keeps you alive. Revealing additional rack tiles disqualifies you: your turn ends without a discard, new exposures return to your rack, earlier valid exposures stay up with their jokers exchangeable, and you still pay the winner at the end. For authoritative rulings, consult the current official NMJL rulebook.

## Examples

- Call, realize, retract before showing tiles: play continues normally.
- Call and lay down your whole hand incorrectly: your hand is dead for the rest of the round.

## Common Mistakes

- Flipping the entire rack before verifying the hand against the card.
- Continuing to draw and discard after being declared dead.
