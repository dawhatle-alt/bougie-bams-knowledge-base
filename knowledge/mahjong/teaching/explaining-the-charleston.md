# Explaining The Charleston

## Summary

The Charleston is easiest to teach as structured hand improvement before regular turns.

## Details

Explain why passing exists before teaching the exact sequence. Use practice tiles and original examples rather than official card content.

## Examples

- Have learners name one tile they are happy to pass and one they want to keep.
- Pause after each pass to review how the hand changed.

## Common Mistakes

- Presenting the Charleston as memorized choreography only.
- Skipping the strategy reason behind passing.
