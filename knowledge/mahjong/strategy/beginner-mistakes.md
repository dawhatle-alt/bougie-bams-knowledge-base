# Beginner Mistakes

## Summary

Most beginner mistakes come from rushing, unclear sorting, or not knowing the current plan.

## Details

Beginners improve quickly when they slow down, name their likely direction, watch discards, and ask rule questions before moving tiles.

## Examples

- A beginner can pause before discarding and ask whether the tile supports any backup plan.
- A teacher can review one mistake after a hand instead of correcting every small hesitation.

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to play entirely from memory.
- Treating every joker or flower as automatically useful.
