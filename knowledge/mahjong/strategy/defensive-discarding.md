# Defensive Discarding

## Summary

Defensive discarding means choosing discards with awareness of what opponents may need.

## Details

Players should watch exposures, repeated discards, and table tempo. Defense does not mean never helping yourself; it means weighing your progress against opponent risk.

## Examples

- A player may avoid discarding a tile that clearly matches an opponent's exposure.
- A teacher can ask what each opponent has shown before a late discard.

## Common Mistakes

- Only staring at your own rack.
- Assuming an earlier safe discard is always safe later.
