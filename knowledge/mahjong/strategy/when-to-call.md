# When To Call

## Summary

A good call should move the hand forward enough to justify revealing information.

## Details

Calling is strongest when it completes a needed group, supports a clear hand direction, or wins the game. Passing is often better when the call exposes a weak plan.

## Examples

- A player near completion may call a tile that creates a needed group.
- A defensive player may decline a call that reveals too much.

## Common Mistakes

- Calling every useful tile.
- Calling without checking whether the hand must remain concealed.
