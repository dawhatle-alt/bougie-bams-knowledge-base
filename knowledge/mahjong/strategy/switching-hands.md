# Switching Hands

## Summary

Switching hands means changing direction when the original plan becomes weak or unlikely.

## Details

A strong player notices when draws, discards, or exposures make a plan less realistic. Beginners should keep a backup path during early play and avoid clinging to a hand that is no longer supported.

## Examples

- A player may pivot from one suit focus to another after receiving helpful tiles.
- An instructor can ask what tiles are shared between two possible paths.

## Common Mistakes

- Switching after every draw.
- Refusing to switch when the table shows the needed tiles are gone.
