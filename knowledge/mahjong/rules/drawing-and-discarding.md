# Drawing And Discarding

## Summary

Drawing adds a tile to the hand; discarding ends the active player's turn in most ordinary situations.

## Details

The draw-and-discard rhythm is the heartbeat of the game. Players should keep drawn tiles controlled, make a clear decision, and place discards where everyone can see them.

## Examples

- A beginner can quietly compare the new tile to their plan before discarding.
- A host can keep a clear discard area in the center of the table.

## Common Mistakes

- Discarding too quickly.
- Mumbling the tile name so other players cannot follow play.
