# Blind Passes and the Courtesy Pass

## Summary

A blind pass lets a player forward unseen tiles on the final pass of either Charleston. The courtesy pass is an optional 0-3 tile exchange across the table after both Charlestons, sized by the player who wants to pass fewer tiles. Jokers may never be passed.

## Details

Blind pass (steal): allowed only on the last pass of the first Charleston (first left) and the last pass of the second Charleston (last right). A player short of unwanted tiles may take one or two of the incoming tiles unseen, combine them with their own, and pass three tiles on.

Courtesy pass: after both Charleston rounds, players seated opposite each other may exchange zero to three tiles. The smaller request controls — if one player offers three and the other offers one, exactly one tile is exchanged.

Stopping the second Charleston: the first Charleston is mandatory; the second is optional and any player may stop it. Intent should be flagged during the first-left pass (asking the table to go slowly). Once a player picks up and looks at tiles from the second round's first pass, it is too late to stop it.

Jokers: jokers may never be passed in any Charleston pass or in the courtesy pass.

## Examples

- Holding only two tiles worth passing on a last-right pass, a player blind-takes one incoming tile to complete their three.
- One player wants a three-tile courtesy exchange, the other wants zero: no tiles are exchanged.

## Common Mistakes

- Blind passing on a middle pass — it is only legal on the final pass of each Charleston.
- Passing a joker during the Charleston, which is never allowed.
