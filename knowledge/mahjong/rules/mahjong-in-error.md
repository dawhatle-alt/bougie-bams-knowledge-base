# Declaring Mahjong in Error

## Summary

The consequences of a false "Mahjong!" call depend on how much the player has exposed: nothing exposed means a free retraction, a single committed exposure means play continues with that exposure locked, and exposing more of the hand means disqualification. Consult the current official NMJL rulebook for authoritative rulings.

## Details

No tiles exposed: the player may retract the declaration without penalty and play continues.

Only the claimed tile or one exposure placed: the player may retract the win declaration, but that exposure stays committed on the rack.

Hand exposed: if the player reveals additional tiles from the rack, the hand is declared dead. The turn ends immediately with no discard, the newly exposed tiles return to the rack, and valid exposures made before the error stay up — their jokers remain exchangeable. Play continues with the next player. If the error followed a claimed discard that another active player could use to win, that player may claim it and win.

A disqualified player stops drawing, discarding, and calling hands dead, but still pays the eventual winner.

Multi-player errors: if several players declare in error and one hand remains intact, the last player to err pays the intact hand double the value of the hand they attempted. A player who throws in their tiles prematurely and destroys the wall pays each intact hand the lowest value on the card.

## Examples

- A player says "Mahjong," realizes the hand is wrong before exposing, and takes it back — no penalty.
- A player exposes their full rack, is declared dead, and sits out the rest of the hand but still pays the winner.

## Common Mistakes

- Exposing the whole hand before double-checking against the card.
- Assuming a dead player's earlier valid exposures lose their jokers — they stay exchangeable.
