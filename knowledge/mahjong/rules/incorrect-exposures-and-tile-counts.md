# Incorrect Exposures and Wrong Tile Counts

## Summary

An invalid exposure can be fixed only until the player discards or exchanges a joker; after that the hand is dead and jokers in the bad exposure are forfeited. A hand with the wrong number of tiles is also dead. Consult the current official NMJL rulebook for authoritative rulings.

## Details

Incorrect exposures: an exposure is invalid when it does not match a legal combination — for example using a joker in a single or pair, exposing on a concealed-only hand, or displaying a pattern not on the card.

- Correction window: the player may fix the exposure during their turn, up until they discard or perform a joker exchange. After that, the exposure is locked.
- If caught later: the hand is declared dead. The incorrect portion returns to the sloped rack, and jokers inside the errant exposure cannot be redeemed by anyone. Valid exposures made earlier stay on the rack with exchangeable jokers.

Wrong tile count: an active hand must hold exactly 13 tiles (14 only momentarily when drawing or winning).

- Too many or too few tiles: the hand is dead. The player stops playing but still pays the winner.
- Three players wrong: the entire hand is voided and redealt.
- During the Charleston: a wrong count found before play begins triggers a full reshuffle and redeal — with one exception: if only the player left of the dealer is missing a tile, they may draw from the wall to correct it before the dealer's first discard.

Wall destruction: a player who prematurely throws in their hand and disturbs the wall pays each remaining intact hand the lowest value on the card.

## Examples

- A player exposes a pung but meant a kong, notices before discarding, and corrects it freely.
- A player found with 12 tiles mid-game is declared dead but remains liable to pay the winner.

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to fix an exposure after discarding — the correction window has closed.
- Redealing the whole game when only the player left of the dealer is short one tile.
