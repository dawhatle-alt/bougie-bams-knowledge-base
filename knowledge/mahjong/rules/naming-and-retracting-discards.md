# Naming and Retracting Discards

## Summary

Discards must be named aloud and placed face up; a tile is locked once fully named or once it touches the table. A misnamed discard penalizes the caller who relies on the wrong name, not the discarder. A discard stays callable until the next player racks a drawn tile.

## Details

Announcing: place the discard face up in the center and clearly name it — "Five Dot," "North," and so on.

When a discard locks: the tile cannot be taken back once it touches the table or has been fully named. A partially named tile ("Five...") that has not touched the table may still be retracted.

Misnaming: if a discarder says the wrong name, the risk falls on players who act on the spoken name. Calling the misnamed tile for an exposure kills the caller's hand; the misnaming discarder faces no penalty. The exception is winning: a mahjong claimed on a misnamed tile stands.

Live vs. dead discards: a discard remains live — available to call — until the next player draws and racks a tile. Once racked, the prior discard is dead forever. If the next player holds their drawn tile without racking it, the earlier discard stays open until they discard or exchange a joker.

## Examples

- A player says "Three Bam" while throwing a Three Crak; an opponent calls it for an exposure of bams and their hand is dead.
- A player begins saying "Six—" and pulls the tile back before it lands: legal retraction.

## Common Mistakes

- Calling a tile based on what was said rather than looking at the tile itself.
- Assuming a discard stays callable after the next player racks their draw.
