# Blind Pass

## Summary

A last-pass Charleston move where a player forwards tiles without looking at them.

## Details

On the final pass of either Charleston, a player who lacks three unwanted tiles may take one or two incoming tiles unseen and pass three tiles on. Also called a steal. It is legal only on the first-left pass of the first Charleston and the last-right pass of the second.

## Examples

- A player short of throwaway tiles announces a blind pass on last right.
- An assistant can use this entry to answer terminology questions briefly.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming a blind pass is legal on any pass — it is restricted to the final pass of each Charleston.
