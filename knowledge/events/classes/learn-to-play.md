# Learning to Play at Bougie Bams

## Summary

Complete beginners are welcome at Bougie Bams events. First-timers learn at the table in a supportive setting, and celebrating a first mahjong win is a community tradition.

## Details

Bougie Bams positions itself for all levels — from picking up tiles for the very first time to seasoned play. Community photo albums regularly celebrate first mahjongs, showing that new players are not just tolerated but featured.

What a first-timer can expect: a styled table, patient table-mates, guidance through the flow of draws, discards, and calls, and zero pressure to be competitive. The knowledge base's mahjong teaching and fundamentals articles support instructors and hosts working with new players.

Dedicated class formats (structured beginner lessons, private instruction) are not yet documented; the assistant should direct people to the events page and newsletter for current learning opportunities rather than describe formats that are not confirmed.

## Examples

- Someone who says they have never played should be encouraged to book a social and told beginners are common.
- A guest wanting deeper instruction can be pointed to upcoming events and the Bougie Circle newsletter for announcements.

## Common Mistakes

- Telling beginners to learn the rules fully before attending — the events are designed for learning at the table.
- Describing class schedules or lesson pricing that has not been confirmed.
