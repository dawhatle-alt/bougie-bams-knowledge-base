# Tile Care Guide

## Summary

Clean acrylic tiles with a soft cloth and mild soapy water, dry them fully before storing, and keep them out of prolonged heat and direct sun to protect the finish.

## Details

- Routine cleaning: wipe tiles with a soft microfiber cloth. For grime, use lukewarm water with a drop of mild dish soap, then dry immediately.
- Avoid: alcohol, acetone, ammonia cleaners, and abrasive pads — these can cloud or scratch acrylic.
- Oils: hands transfer oils during play; a quick wipe-down after game night keeps engraving paint bright.
- Storage: store tiles dry, in their case, away from hot cars and windowsills. Heat can warp acrylic and fade engraving color.
- Travel: pad the case so tiles do not rattle face-to-face, which can chip edges over time.

## Examples

- After a long event, a host can wipe tiles down before racking them back into the case.
- A customer asking how to remove sunscreen residue should use mild soapy water, not solvent wipes.

## Common Mistakes

- Using disinfectant wipes with alcohol, which dulls the acrylic surface.
- Storing sets in a hot garage or car trunk in Texas summers.
