# Choosing a Tile Set

## Summary

When choosing a mahjong tile set, weigh four things: material and hand-feel, engraving crispness, whether it is configured for American mahjong (jokers and blanks included), and the aesthetic you want on your table.

## Details

Material and feel: high-grade acrylic tiles offer satisfying weight, a pleasant click, and durability. Bougie Bams curates sets built from premium acrylics and metals, hand-finished for consistent polish.

American mahjong readiness: American play requires jokers, so confirm any set includes joker tiles and ideally spare blanks. Standard American sets run 166 tiles.

Engraving: crisp, deeply engraved characters stay readable for years and are easier for beginners to distinguish across the table.

Aesthetics: tile color, engraving color, and case design set the tone of the table. Bougie Bams leans into modern, design-forward looks that pair with its mats.

Size matters too: larger tiles are easier to read and handle, but confirm they fit your racks.

## Examples

- A beginner buying a first set should prioritize readable engraving and a full American configuration with jokers.
- A customer matching a Pink Snake Bandana mat can choose a tile color that complements the mat design.

## Common Mistakes

- Buying a Chinese-style set without jokers for American play.
- Choosing on looks alone and ending up with lightweight tiles that feel flimsy.
