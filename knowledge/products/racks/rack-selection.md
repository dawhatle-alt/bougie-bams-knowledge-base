# Choosing Racks and Pushers

## Summary

A full table needs four racks, ideally with attached pushers. Choose racks that fit your tile size, feel stable on the table, and complement your mat and set aesthetically.

## Details

Function: racks hold each player's hand upright and hide it from opponents; the top shelf displays exposures. Pushers — the arms attached to many modern racks — square up and push out the wall cleanly during the deal.

What to check:

- Tile fit: oversized tiles need deeper rack channels. Confirm compatibility before mixing brands.
- Pushers: combined rack-and-pusher designs speed up the deal and are the standard at styled events.
- Stability: heavier racks stay put when players draw and rack tiles quickly.
- Style: rack color is a styling decision; at Bougie Bams events, racks are matched to the mat and tile palette as part of the tablescape.

## Examples

- A customer building a first table needs four racks plus their tile set and mat.
- A customer with oversized tiles should confirm rack channel depth before purchasing.

## Common Mistakes

- Buying three racks because one player "can hold tiles in hand" — always four.
- Mixing rack brands with tiles that do not seat properly in the channel.
