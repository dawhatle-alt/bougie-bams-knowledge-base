# Mat Sizing Guide

## Summary

Choose the 33 inch mat for full-size tables and the most generous play area, the 31 inch for standard card tables and smaller dining tables, and the 20 inch travel mat for portability.

## Details

- 33 inch: the roomiest option. Best when the table comfortably fits four players with space for racks, discards, and drinks. The go-to for a dedicated game table.
- 31 inch: fits standard folding card tables and most four-seat dining tables while still holding the full wall and discard area comfortably.
- 20 inch travel: compact and packable for trips, patios, and casual play. Expect a tighter layout; it suits two to four players who do not mind a cozier table.

Measure the usable flat surface of the table before choosing. Leave at least a few inches of table edge beyond the mat for racks and elbows.

## Examples

- A customer with a 34 inch square card table should choose the 31 inch mat so racks fit outside the mat edge.
- A customer who plays at a country club with large tables can size up to 33 inch.

## Common Mistakes

- Buying a mat the same width as the table, leaving no room for racks.
- Choosing the travel size for a permanent setup and finding the play area tight.
